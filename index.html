Ellora Yasi

Hello! My name is Ellora, and I am a Computer Science graduate from the Military Institute of Science and Technology (MIST), with a passion for coding, problem-solving, and continuous learning.

During my time at MIST, I was actively involved with the MIST Computer Club and served as the Executive Director of the Committee of Competitive Programming in my final year. I participated in numerous programming contests, including national-level competitions like NGPC and NCPC, which helped me inspire juniors and develop my critical thinking and problem-solving skills. Additionally, I served as Vice President for Content Writing and Promotions at the MIST Cyber Security Club, where we organized flag-hunting competitions and seminars.

I also completed an internship with Trust Bank Limited, where I worked in their In-House Software Development Team as part of my industrial attachment in the 4th year.

I have experience working with machine learning models for real-world applications such as flood forecasting and data generation. My thesis focused on predicting floods in Bangladesh, a country frequently impacted by catastrophic flooding. We utilized both tabular and image data for our predictions, and you can view a demo of our work.

In addition to my technical interests, I enjoy playing Rubik's Cube, chess, and badminton. I also owned a team in MIST’s intra-department cricket and football tournaments.

I am currently working at the Military Institute of Science and Technology (MIST) as a Research Assistant, focusing on machine learning (ML), deep learning (DL), and model deployment. I thrive on challenges, enjoy building solutions, and am always eager to learn new skills and technologies.
